<p><p>The polymer gel market is categorized into two main types: ionic and non-ionic gels. Ionic polymer gels contain charged groups that interact with ions in the surrounding medium, making them responsive to changes in ionic strength and pH. These gels are often used in applications such as drug delivery and sensors. Non-ionic polymer gels, on the other hand, lack charged groups, resulting in a more stable structure, making them suitable for use in cosmetics, food, and personal care products. Both types offer unique advantages tailored to specific applications.</p></p>
