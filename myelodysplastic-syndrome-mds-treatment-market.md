<p><p>Myelodysplastic Syndrome (MDS) is a group of heterogeneous bone marrow disorders characterized by ineffective hematopoiesis, leading to blood cell deficiencies. Treatment options for MDS include supportive care, disease-modifying therapies, and hematopoietic stem cell transplantation. Supportive care primarily focuses on managing symptoms and may involve transfusions, growth factors, and antibiotics. Disease-modifying treatments, such as hypomethylating agents and chemotherapeutic drugs, aim to improve blood counts and decrease the risk of progression to acute myeloid leukemia. Emerging therapies, including targeted treatments and novel immunotherapies, are being explored to enhance patient outcomes.</p><p>The Myelodysplastic Syndrome (MDS) Treatment Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 12.5% during the forecast period. Collaborations among pharmaceutical companies and research institutions are also shaping the competitive landscape, enhancing the overall landscape of MDS treatment options globally.</p></p>
