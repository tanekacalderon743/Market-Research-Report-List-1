<p><p>The metal casing market encompasses various manufacturing techniques, including stamping, die casting, extrusion, and CNC machining. Stamping involves shaping metal sheets into desired forms using dies, commonly used for lightweight components. Die casting enables the production of intricate shapes through molten metal injection into molds, ideal for high-volume applications. Extrusion involves forcing molten metal through a die to create long shapes with consistent cross-sections. CNC machining offers precision shaping of metal casings by removing material from solid blocks, ensuring detailed and complex designs.</p></p>
