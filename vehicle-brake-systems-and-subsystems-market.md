<p><p>Vehicle brake systems are crucial for safety and performance, primarily classified into drum and disc brake types. Drum brakes consist of a cylindrical drum and brake shoes that press against the drum's inner surface, providing strong braking force. Disc brakes utilize a flat disc and caliper that grips the disc with brake pads, offering superior heat dissipation and consistent performance. Both systems cater to different vehicle types, with disc brakes commonly found on modern vehicles due to their effectiveness and reliability in various conditions.</p></p>
